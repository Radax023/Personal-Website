Introducion

Hello there! I’m Reza Adousi, currently a second-year Computer Engineering student at Khatam University. Ever since I remember, I’ve been obsessed with computer-coding, making games, and basically building cool stuff. Back in high school at Allameh Helli, one of Iran’s selective schools, I started diving into creative projects—and I haven’t really stopped since. I’m what you might call a “one-dimensional hermit”: you’ll mostly find behind the computer. Catch me outside? Rarely… unless I’m at the gym, pretending to exercise. I love dreaming up new concepts, creating AI-powered projects, and constantly leveling up my knowledge in tech. Oh, and when I’m not coding, you’ll probably find me shredding my electric guitar.

Allame Helli School

Back in high school, I studied at Allameh Helli, which is one of Iran’s “NODET schools” (basically, elite schools for talented students). Sounds fancy, right? Well… it was fancy torture! The years there were intense—think endless studying, everyday exams, and an atmosphere where everyone’s life goal was surviving the Konkoor (Iran’s national university entrance exam). If you don’t know what Konkoor is: imagine SAT, ACT, GRE, and Dark Souls combined into one monster test… that decides your entire future. What I really got out of it? Besides the traumas, I got amazing friends, some solid discipline, and the habit of overthinking everything. So yeah, it was tough, but also kinda cool.

Khatam University

Khatam University is a private university in Tehran, founded by the Pasargad Bank. It’s known for having modern programs and small classes — meaning the professors actually know who you are (which can be good or terrifying, depending on your grades). Sure, it’s not MIT, but it’s got something better: people. Honestly, the students here are the real upgrade—you sign up for classes, but what you really get is a crew of good-hearted legends.
